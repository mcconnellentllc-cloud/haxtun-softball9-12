Fielding

For 9-12-year-olds, fielding is about getting in front of the ball, fielding with two hands out in front, and using quick feet to get to the ball. Build a reliable ready position and "soft hands" first; range and speed come later. Short, focused reps with good form beat long, sloppy ones.

Written drill

Triangle Ground Balls

Glove, a few balls, partner 10 min
  1. Start in an athletic ready position: feet wider than shoulders, knees bent, glove out front.
  2. Partner rolls a grounder right at you.
  3. Field the ball out in front so your two feet and your glove make a triangle.
  4. Use two hands — "alligator" the ball, top hand covering it — and funnel it to your belly button.
  5. Toss it back and reset. Aim for 10-15 clean reps.
Written drill

Short-Hop Soft Hands

Glove, ball, partner or wall 8 min
  1. Kneel or stay low about 10-15 feet from a partner (or a wall).
  2. Partner throws short hops — balls that bounce just in front of your glove.
  3. Catch the ball right as it comes off the bounce ("short hop"), giving softly with your hands.
  4. Keep the glove out front and watch the ball all the way in.
  5. Do 15-20 reps; this builds confidence on tough in-between hops.

Pitching

Windmill pitching is built one piece at a time: balance, a relaxed full arm circle, and a snappy wrist release. At 9-12, work on clean mechanics and hitting the target from a comfortable distance — speed follows good form. Keep sessions short and stop before the arm gets tired.

Written drill

Wrist Snaps

Ball, partner or net 5 min
  1. Stand about 10 feet from a partner, ball in your throwing hand.
  2. Hold your forearm steady (you can brace it with your glove hand).
  3. Snap only your wrist to flip the ball with backspin into your partner's glove.
  4. Listen for the ball to "zip" — that's good spin.
  5. Do 20-25 snaps to wake up the release.
Written drill

Single-Knee Arm Circle

Ball, target or partner 8 min
  1. Kneel on your throwing-side knee, glove-side knee up.
  2. Make one smooth, full arm circle close to your ear and hip.
  3. Finish with a strong wrist snap toward the target.
  4. This takes the legs out so you can feel a clean arm path.
  5. Throw 15-20, resetting your grip and balance each time.
Written drill

Walk-Through Power Line

Ball, flat ground or a line 10 min
  1. Pick a straight line (chalk, a seam in the driveway) pointing at your target.
  2. Slowly walk through the pitch: step, arm circle, stride, snap, finish — staying on the line.
  3. Keep your stride foot landing on the line, not across it.
  4. Repeat slowly for control, then add a little speed once it feels smooth.

Base Running

Smart, aggressive base running wins close games. At this age, focus on running hard through first base, rounding bases with a good turn, and the fastpitch "leadoff" — remember, a runner can't leave the base until the pitcher releases the ball. Teach players to watch the ball and always know how many outs there are.

Written drill

Run Through First

A base or cone 5 min
  1. Start at "home" and sprint to first base.
  2. Run all the way through the bag — don't slow down or jump.
  3. Hit the front edge of the base and keep going a few steps.
  4. Peek to your right (toward foul ground) for an overthrow.
  5. Reset and repeat 5-6 times at full speed.
Written drill

Banana Turn

2-3 bases or cones 8 min
  1. Sprinting toward a base you'll round, drift slightly outside in a small "banana" curve a few steps before it.
  2. Touch the inside corner of the base with either foot.
  3. Lean your shoulders in toward the next base as you push off.
  4. Practice home-to-second and first-to-third turns.
Written drill

Leadoff Timing

A base, a partner to "pitch" 6 min
  1. Stand on the base in an athletic stance, eyes on the pitcher.
  2. As the pitcher's hand releases the ball, push off and take 2-3 hard steps off the base.
  3. Stay low and balanced, ready to go on a passed ball or to get back.
  4. Repeat so leaving "on release" becomes automatic (you can't leave early in fastpitch).

Throwing

Accurate, repeatable overhand throwing starts with the grip and the arm path. At 9-12, build a four-seam grip, a glove-side point at the target, and a full follow-through. Play catch with a purpose every time, and grow arm strength gradually — never throw through pain.

Written drill

Four-Seam Grip Catch

Ball, partner 8 min
  1. Grip the ball across the wide part of the seams ("C" or horseshoe) with two or three fingers.
  2. Every time you catch, find that grip before you throw.
  3. Throw with backspin so the ball stays straight and true.
  4. Play catch for 15-20 throws, re-gripping each time.
Written drill

"T" Power Position

Ball, partner 8 min
  1. Stand sideways to your target, arms in a "T": throwing hand back with ball, glove pointing at the target.
  2. Lead with your glove, then pull it to your chest as your throwing arm comes through.
  3. Finish with your throwing hand past your opposite hip.
  4. Start close, then take a step back every few good throws.
Written drill

Distance Ladder

Ball, partner or wall 10 min
  1. Start close and make 5 accurate throws on a line.
  2. Take a few steps back and make 5 more.
  3. Keep stepping back only when throws stay accurate.
  4. Then walk it back in, finishing with crisp, easy throws.

Situational Awareness

The best young players think before the pitch. At 9-12, the goal is simple habits: know the number of outs, know where you'll throw the ball on a grounder or a fly, hit the cutoff, and back up your teammates. Say it out loud — talking through plays builds game IQ fast.

Written drill

"Before the Pitch" Call-Out

No gear — just talk 5 min
  1. Before each pretend pitch, the player says the number of outs out loud.
  2. Then: "If it's a grounder, I throw to ___." and "If it's a fly ball, I ___."
  3. A parent calls a new situation (runners/outs) each time.
  4. Repeat until the answers come quickly and confidently.
Written drill

Hit the Cutoff

2-3 players, gloves, balls 10 min
  1. One player plays "outfield," one is the cutoff, one covers a base.
  2. Roll a ball to the outfielder; they field and throw chest-high to the cutoff.
  3. The cutoff catches, turns glove-side, and relays to the base.
  4. Emphasize a quick, accurate, low throw — not the longest throw.
Written drill

Know Where to Throw

Infield space, a few players 12 min
  1. Set a situation: "Runner on first, one out."
  2. Roll or hit a ground ball; the fielder makes the smart play (often the lead-runner force or the sure out).
  3. Talk through why that was the right base.
  4. Change the situation and repeat — outs, runners, score.
