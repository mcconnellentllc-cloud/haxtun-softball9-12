Player by Player

#1 — Kyla W.

Suited up for 10 of 11 games and turned 7 walks into a .571 OBP — the table-setter when she got into the box. Drove in 3 runs, scored 2. Plate discipline was a real weapon at the top of the lineup.

#2 — Pyper J.

Team-leading 6 RBI on just 3 hits — every hit drove someone home. Started all 11 games. Perfect 1.000 fielding (3 chances, 3 outs) and 5 walks of her own. The team's most efficient run producer.

#3 — Kortlyn C.

The ace. Team-high 39 strikeouts in 16 innings — most by a wide margin — across 6 pitching appearances. Also hit .250 with 4 walks and 2 HBP for a .571 OBP, and fielded a perfect 1.000 from the circle. Did it on both sides.

#4 — Amber E.

A workhorse. Played 10 games and made the second-most pitching appearances (7 outings, 10 IP, 23 K). Also one of the busiest fielders on the team (14 chances, 10 putouts). Took 4 HBP on the way to a .526 OBP, scored 5, drove in 4. Whatever the team needed.

#5 — Victoria C.

Got into 4 games and made every one count: a walk and an HBP in 4 PA — a .500 OBP. Gave the dugout depth and a fresh bat when called on.

#6 — Waylyn H.

Started all 11 games. Three hits, 4 RBI, 5 runs scored — and 9 chances in the outfield. The kind of consistent presence that doesn't always show up in the box score.

#7 — Oakley M.

Ironwoman. 22 PA across 11 games — drew 11 walks (2nd on team) and 5 RBI, with a perfect 1.000 fielding on 8 chances. Battled deep counts all year long.

#8 — Aisley F.

Five games, one hit, two walks. Brought energy when she came in and made the most of her opportunities.

#9 — Delaney P.

Team-best OBP at .643 (min 10 PA) — 9 walks in 14 plate appearances. Almost impossible to get out. Scored 2 runs and gave the next hitters a runner to drive in.

#11 — Crisbel G.

Six games and 2 HBP — wore them for the team. Battled through 5 at-bats and never gave away a plate appearance.

#16 — Tayla M.

Eight games, an RBI, and 2 walks. Made a putout in the field for a perfect 1.000 FPCT. Steady role player.

#20 — Palmer H.

The defensive bedrock. 81 fielding chances, 81 putouts, 1.000 FPCT — more than any other Bulldog by a country mile. Also team-leading 12 walks and 7 runs scored. When the Bulldogs needed an out at first, Palmer was the answer every time.

#21 — Phoenix B.

One of the season's biggest stories. Team-leading 10 runs scored, 12 stolen bases (12-for-13), and the only home run of the season. Best ERA on the pitching staff at 4.500 with 14 K in 6.2 IP. At the plate: .357 / .625 / 1.196 — one of three players over a 1.000 OPS. MVP-caliber summer.

#22 — Tyndle M.

Set the tone everywhere. Team-leading 8 hits, .421 AVG, .943 OPS (most among players with 10+ ABs). Pitched 6 innings and played the team's busiest infield — 9 chances, 6 assists (most on the team). The total package.

#23

The team's highest AVG (.444) and OPS (1.421) — both team highs by a wide margin. 4 hits in 9 ABs, including the tournament's only Bulldog hit (a triple). 4 RBI, 4 walks. Quietly the team's best pure hitter when she was in the box.

#24 — Maddie B.

Five games, took an HBP, and was ready whenever her number got called. Showed up.

How they helped the team

Every name above is a thread in the story. The walks (74 of them — almost two free passes per inning on average) came from the patient at-bats Oakley, Palmer, Delaney, and Kyla put together. The runs scored came when Phoenix, Palmer, Waylyn, Amber, and Tyndle turned those baserunners into runs. The defense held together behind Palmer's vacuum at first, Tyndle and Kortlyn's gloves up the middle, and Waylyn and Amber backing them in the outfield. And the pitching — when Kortlyn, Amber, Phoenix, and Tyndle took the circle — kept the Bulldogs in games they had no business being in.

Two wins. A handful of close losses. A first-ever home run. A bracket appearance. A roster that grew up together over six weeks.

Thank you, Bulldogs. And thank you to the families and coaches who made the season possible.
