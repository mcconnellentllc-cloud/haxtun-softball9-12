Quick reference for 9-12 girls. Rules marked 9-12 Only apply only to our age group. For everything else, see the NFHS Rules page.
1

Pitching distances: Girls 9-12 — 35 feet. Girls 13-16 — 43 feet.

2

Base paths: 60 feet. 9-12 uses an 11" ball. 13-16 uses a 12" ball.

3

Game length and time limit: Girls 9-12 play 5 innings with a 1 hour 15 minute time limit. Girls 13-16 play 5 innings with a 1 hour 30 minute time limit.

Run rule: 20 after 3, 15 after 4, 10 after 5.

4

Suspended game: If a game is called due to weather, it resumes from the point of interruption.

5

Stealing: A runner may steal after the ball leaves the pitcher's hand. Runners cannot lead off.

6

Stealing home 9-12 Only: A runner can steal home or another base if the catcher attempts to pick off a runner at one of the bases or throws over to the pitcher.

7

Dropped third strike 9-12 Only: The runner cannot advance to first on a dropped third strike.

8

Sliding: Sliding is not mandatory. However, if the runner obstructs the play, the umpire may call the runner out.

9

Courtesy runners: May only be used for the pitcher and catcher.

10

Nine-batter half innings 9-12 Only: Only nine batters will hit in the half inning.

The ninth batter gets a normal at-bat. If she is walked or hit by pitch, she takes first. If the bases are loaded, that results in a run; if not, the inning is over and runners cannot advance. A strikeout ends the inning. If the ball is put in play, the ball must be brought to home plate and home plate touched for the inning to end.

Extra hitters (both age divisions): You may have 3 Extra Hitters. These 3 players hit in the batting order. The 12 players can also be freely substituted defensively. A substitution other than these 12 is subject to the normal substitution rules.

11

Bat throwing: Throwing the bat at the end of a swing is an automatic out after one team warning. Intentionally throwing the bat is an automatic ejection after one team warning.

12

Catcher's gear: Catchers must wear full protective gear — mask, chest protector, and shin guards.

13

Batting helmets: Must be worn at all times while the batter is on the playing field.

14

Steel cleats: Allowed for 13-16 games only. Not allowed for 9-12.

15

Tournament eligibility: Players must play in one more than half of the league games to play in the tournament. Exceptions require approval by majority vote of league representatives. Players can only play out of their age grouping or between softball and baseball if those teams are short of players.

17

Ejections: If a player or coach is ejected, they are not eligible to play or coach for one calendar week from the date of ejection. A second ejection in the same season ends their season — no further play or coaching.

18

Tournament championship: No time limit on Championship games in the tournament.

For everything not listed above, refer to the NFHS rulebook. See our NFHS Rules page for a quick-reference summary and links to the official sources.

Rule Clarifications for Fans

Some of the most common in-game calls that catch families off guard. Each clarifies what the rule is and what the penalty looks like.

A

Lookback Rule — when the runner has to return (NFHS 8-7)

Once the pitcher has the ball inside the pitching circle, any runner off her base must commit one direction immediately — either keep going to the next base or get back to her base. She can't stop, hesitate, or reverse direction.

Penalty: the runner is out.

(A foul ball that's not caught is a dead ball — runners simply return to their previous base with no penalty.)

B

Taunting the pitcher (NFHS 3-6, unsporting conduct)

A batter, baserunner, or coach cannot taunt, bait, or otherwise try to distract the pitcher. Umpire's judgment call.

Penalty: a verbal warning first. Repeated or severe behavior is an ejection. An ejection triggers our league Rule 17 — out one calendar week from the date of ejection; a second ejection in the same season ends the player's or coach's season.

C

Stepping out of the batter's box while swinging (NFHS 7-3-2)

At the moment the bat contacts the ball, the batter must have at least part of one foot inside the batter's box (or touching the chalk line). If her entire foot is on the ground completely outside the box — or any part of her foot is touching home plate — when she hits the ball, she's done.

Penalty: the batter is out. The ball is dead and no runners advance.

D

Batting out of order (NFHS 7-1-1, 7-1-2)

The defensive team must appeal before the next pitch for any penalty. Three windows:

While the wrong batter is still hitting: the correct batter takes over and inherits the count (balls/strikes). No out.

After the wrong batter finishes, before the next pitch: the correct batter (the one who should have hit) is called out, runs and advances from the improper at-bat are wiped, and the next batter is the one after the called-out player in the order.

After the next pitch to the following batter: too late — the at-bat stands, and the order picks up from there.

Penalty: up to one out plus erased advances, depending on when the appeal is made.
